Archetypal Consulting

The Power of Transformation and Healing

Have you wondered why am I here? What is my soul’s purpose? Maybe there are people in your life that seem to have it all “figured out” – they seem to know what they were born to do or how to “be.” And although work, relationships, and personal goals are important, maybe you have wondered: “Is that it?” When we stop to reflect, we know, at a deep inner level, that life is not that simple. In order to be able to answer those questions, we need the right language to excavate that answer from deep within ourselves. Because it is more than a question - it is a symbol. It symbolizes a maturity that has us shifting from defining ourselves by what we do or own to wanting to define ourselves by what we be or contribute. We don’t ask that question unless we really want to understand the reason we were given this gift of life.

I became engrossed with archetypes since I first read Women Who Run with Wolves by Clarissa Pinkola Estes. Longing for more, I went deeper into mythology, archetypes and “Conscious Femininity” with Jungian Analyst Marion Woodman. However, it was in reading Caroline Myss’s work with Sacred Contracts that I was really able to embody archetypes into every aspect of my life. Diving deep into this work, I was shocked to learn that my archetypes had been shaping my life from the moment I was born. By identifying and exploring my archetypes, I came to understand my true self. I learned why some types of clothing I wore, why some people I met, and why some relationships felt so right and others brought such discomfort. I learned to not only ask myself but also be able to answer – does this fit into who I am? Am I living the life I was destined to live?

This is why I love archetypes, because they awaken you to the signs and symbols that come into your life and bring meaning to them. Once you know and understand your archetypes you begin to intuitively listen to them as they send out messages of blessings or warnings.This is true empowerment.

Is there an inner voice, a restlessness in your body or soul, or maybe you are feeling inspired – whatever the prompt is, you are here. This transformational journey will teach you to know and understand your archetypes, how they influence your thoughts, attitude, and behavior. It will provide a greater understanding of what motivates you, the obstacles and habits that hold you back, and support in integrating meaningful change in your life. With a courageous heart this path will deepen courage, spirituality, and empowerment. Discovering your archetypes is not just a package of knowledge and methods that can be intellectually memorized and grasped. This path involves deep inner work that cannot be forced. It takes time, contemplation, and discernment.

Archetypes are a symbolic language of the universe and act as a map to help us understand life patterns. Dwelling in the deeper layers of our unconscious, these are patterns that drive us and influence the quality of our lives. Archetypes are the all-inclusive patterns of the human psyche: instinct, impulse, survival, fate, destiny and soul. They not only structure our behavior, they also influence our imagination, perception, ideas and choices. Beyond the limits of our rational and linear mind, archetypes give us access to the deeper levels of insight, wisdom and creativity in our unconscious.

It is important to know that Archetypes are neutral, but have a light and shadow quality and are both creative and destructive. The term ‘shadow,’ when used in regards to archetypes, refers to all that is unknown or yet to be known in you. It’s not meant to imply negative, dark, bad or wrong. The shadow has power precisely because it remains in the dark; we tend to deny its presence in us because we consider it unacceptable. Yet it is often the unknown parts of ourselves that cause us the most chaos in our lives. Only when we face and acknowledge the shadow’s presence can we neutralize its potential negative impact on us. In addition, archetypes aren’t literal. They are symbolic and metaphorical. To make meaning, we can’t enter through logic. We must go through symbols. It’s through entering the symbolic realm that we gain access to the power of archetypes. Lastly, while archetypes are ancient, impersonal and universal, they become personalized when our individual psyche engages them. For example, they are impersonal in the sense that you aren’t an addict but the pattern of addiction is active in you.
